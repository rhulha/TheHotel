VILLAIN IDEAS — The Hotel

1. THE CONCIERGE / THE MASTER FIGURE
The face of the Hotel — impeccable, warm, almost paternal. He hired Nate; he
remembers everyone's name; he's the one who recites "what happens in the Hotel
stays in the Hotel" like a blessing. Not a screaming tyrant — he BELIEVES in the
system, genuinely thinks draining Universe1 to sustain Universe2 is mercy ("at
least someone gets to live well"). The horror is how reasonable he sounds. The
Krabat "Master" — the institution given a kind face.
Best as: the recurring on-page antagonist Nate has to break with emotionally.

2. NATE'S OWN TWIN (THE COUNTERPART VILLAIN)
The Universe2 Nate — same face, but he was conceived on the lucky side and grew
up in paradise. Everything Nate could have been: comfortable, educated,
untouchable. And he's fine with the arrangement; he may even work for the Hotel
from the other side, processing the resources that keep his world soft. The most
personal villain possible: Nate's enemy is proof that he isn't special, just
unlucky — and that he might have done the same in those shoes.
Pairs with: the mirror-confrontation climax.

3. THE FOUNDER / "PATIENT ZERO"
The pre-branch elite who OPENED the portal — the one person alive in Universe1
who remembers the single world before it split, and who engineered the whole
siphon. Ancient by influence if not by age. Because they were born before the
branch, they have a twin in Universe2 — and the dark reveal: they murdered/caged
that twin long ago to consolidate power across both worlds. The architect of the
dystopia, hiding inside the Bilderberg crowd as just another guest.


---

- Institutional: the Concierge (the seductive human face Nate must reject — the
                 Krabat Master).

- Personal:      Nate's twin (the intimate mirror that makes it about him).

Solution: Both. The Concierge is the face of the system, but the real villain is the Nate2.
