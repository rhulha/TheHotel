I want to have a book about a guy (Nate) trying to get hired by a Hotel. He is desperately looking for a job.

The prose has to be in the style of Peter Clines.

Nate is a down-on-his-luck guy in his early thirties, struggling to make ends meet in a city that never seems to sleep. After months of fruitless job hunting, he finally gets a lead from an old friend about a prestigious hotel that is always looking for discreet and reliable staff. Intrigued by the opportunity, Nate decides to pursue it, despite the lack of information about the establishment.

The Hotel is very secretive and prestigious and they don't advertise. A friend told him about it.

The Hotel has a dark secret that Nate uncovers over the course of the book.

It hosts people like the Bilderberg Meeting.

Nate is told to be 100% discreet about the Hotel and its guests.
This is the number one rule of the Hotel.
What happens in the Hotel stays in the Hotel. No exceptions.

Nate quickly learns that the Hotel is not just a place for the wealthy to rest their heads—it's a hub of power, influence, and secrecy. As he navigates the labyrinthine halls and meets the enigmatic staff and guests, he begins to uncover the dark underbelly of the establishment.

In the cellar is a hidden room with a portal. This portal leads to Universe2,
a branch of Universe1, split off via the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) of quantum mechanics.

Universe1 is a dystopian society where healthcare is not free and education comes with unforgivable debt. People can't afford housing and public debt is crushing and inflation is out of control.

In Universe2 life is great. The USA has universal healthcare and college is free.

The way this is paid for is by people going through the portal into Universe1 and stealing the resources. 

The people in Universe1 are struggling to survive while their resources are siphoned off to support the lavish lifestyles of those in Universe2.

The hidden room in the cellar is a surreal space.

Nate first hears about the room from a fellow employee who warns him to stay away from it. Curiosity gets the better of him, and one night, he decides to explore the cellar.

He gets to know other employees who are also curious.

Together they decide to investigate the room.

As Nate steps into the hidden room, he is transported to Universe2, where he witnesses the stark contrast between the two worlds.

There, he sees the consequences of the exploitation carried out by the Hotel and its powerful clientele. 

The more he learns, the more dangerous his situation becomes, as powerful figures will stop at nothing to keep their secrets safe.
