The "Dark Night of the Soul" is the beat right after All Is Lost — not the event itself but the protagonist sitting in the wreckage of it, having given up, before he finds the spark that launches the climax. It's internal, quiet, despairing. 

Drinking alone in the room they gave him
Nate sits in his small staff quarters inside the Hotel — the job he was once desperate for now a gilded cell. He looks at his first paycheck, or the staff uniform, and understands the awful trade he made: he stopped being broke by becoming part of the machine. He almost burns the evidence. The temptation isn't to fight — it's to let it go and keep the bed and the meals.

. The phone call he can't make
He pulls up the old friend who got him the lead — the one who said "trust me, it's a good gig" — and realizes either (a) the friend knew all along and fed him to it, or (b) the friend is now unreachable/dead.  OR HE IS THE BAD GUY


 The math of complicity
He tries to justify it to himself: Universe2 has free healthcare, free college, people live well — maybe the suffering on the other side is worth it, maybe I'm the naive one. For a long, ugly moment he almost believes the Hotel's logic. The dark night is him nearly being converted — losing not just hope but his moral compass.
